Planned Summer 2022 Visit

We are currently planning the Old Foleyans’ Association summer visit to the National Horse Racing Museum in Newmarket.

Details will be published by the end of March. We are hoping that it will happen on the afternoon of Sunday 29th May 2022, one week ahead of the Queen’s Jubilee Bank Holiday weekend.

National Horseracing Museum

The National Horse Racing Museum is a five-acre site in the heart of Newmarket. It comprises the following three complementary attractions:

  • The National Horse Racing Museum in the Trainer’s House and King’s Yard Galleries.
  • The Packard Galleries of British Sporting Art in Palace House.
  • The chance to meet former racehorses in the flagship home of Retraining of Racehorses.

There is also a fabulous restaurant and gift shop on-site.

In November 2021 the Museum was delighted to be awarded a Gold Accolade by VisitEngland. Seventy-six attractions around England were recognised as a result of the exceptional experiences they provide visitors from Welcome Accolade, Hidden Gem, Best Told Story, Quality Food and Drink, and the Gold Accolade for all-round top-scoring attractions.

We are committed to doing everything we can to protect each other as we ease out of COVID restrictions and our primary concern remains the safety and wellbeing of our staff, volunteers and visitors. We continue to have COVID-secure guidelines in place whilst acknowledging the change from legislation to personal responsibility. We know that people are looking forward to this relaxation, but appreciate there is nervousness amongst some audiences, particularly those with access requirements or those who feel more vulnerable. We aim to create the best and safest environment for all and encourage the wearing of face coverings in crowded indoor areas where it is difficult to socially distance from others, as well as test and trace, plus a number of protocols that protect our staff and all our visitors when on site.
